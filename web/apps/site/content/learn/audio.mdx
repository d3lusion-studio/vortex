The short form covers most of what a game needs:

```cpp
app.playSound("assets/jump.wav");
app.playSound("assets/music.ogg", /*loop*/ true);
```

The sound is loaded on first use and reused afterwards, keyed by path.

## The engine

For anything more — stopping a loop, per-sound volume — take the engine:

```cpp
audio::IAudioEngine* engine = app.audio();
if (!engine) return;   // see below

const audio::SoundHandle music = engine->load("assets/music.ogg");

engine->play(music, /*loop*/ true);
engine->setVolume(music, 0.4f);
engine->setMasterVolume(0.8f);
engine->stop(music);
engine->unload(music);
```

<Callout type="warn">
  **`app.audio()` can return `nullptr`, and that is not an error.** A machine with no audio device —
  a CI runner, a headless server, a container — will not have one, and the engine's answer is to keep
  running silently rather than refuse to start.

  Check the pointer. `playSound()` already does, and is simply a no-op with no device, which is why
  it is the safer call.
</Callout>

The device is opened on the **first** call to `audio()` or `playSound()`, and a failed open is not
retried every frame. A game that makes no sound never opens a device at all.
